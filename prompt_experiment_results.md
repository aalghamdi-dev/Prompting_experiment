# Prompt Engineering Experiment Results

Model used: `google/gemini-2.5-flash-lite`

## 1. Basic

**Prompt:**
> Write an email asking for a deadline extension in 120 words.

**Response:**

Subject: Request for Extension - [Your Name] - [Project Name/Task]

Dear [Recipient Name],

I am writing to respectfully request a short extension for the [Project Name/Task] deadline, currently set for [Original Due Date].

Due to [briefly and honestly state reason, e.g., unforeseen technical difficulties, unexpected workload increase, needing more time for thorough review], I require a little more time to ensure the quality of my submission.

I would be grateful if the deadline could be extended to [Proposed New Due Date]. I am confident I can deliver the completed work by this new date.

Thank you for your understanding and consideration.

Sincerely,
 
[Your Name]

## 2. Improved

**Prompt:**
> Write a polite email to my professor asking for a 3-day extension on my assignment because I am sick. Keep it 120 words.

**Response:**

Subject: Extension Request - [Your Name] - [Course Name] - [Assignment Name]

Dear Professor [Professor's Last Name],

I am writing to respectfully request a three-day extension for the [Assignment Name] assignment, currently due on [Original Due Date].

Unfortunately, I have fallen ill and am experiencing [briefly mention symptom, e.g., flu-like symptoms] which has significantly impacted my ability to focus and complete the assignment to the best of my ability.

I would be very grateful if I could have until [New Proposed Due Date] to submit my work. I apologize for any inconvenience this may cause and appreciate your understanding.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,

## 3. Detailed

**Prompt:**
> You are a university student writing to your professor. Write a polite, respectful email requesting a 3-day extension on your Data Structures assignment due to a documented illness. Mention that you have already completed 70 percent of the work and will submit as soon as possible. Keep it in the range of 120 words.

**Response:**

Subject: Extension Request - [Your Name] - Data Structures Assignment

Dear Professor [Professor's Last Name],

I hope this email finds you well.

I am writing to respectfully request a three-day extension for the upcoming Data Structures assignment, currently due on [Original Due Date]. Unfortunately, I have been unwell for the past few days, and this has impacted my ability to complete the assignment by the deadline. I have attached a doctor's note for your reference.

I have made significant progress and have approximately 70 percent of the assignment completed. I am committed to submitting my best work and will do so as soon as I am able, aiming for [Proposed New Due Date - 3 days after original].

Thank you for your understanding and consideration.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
[Your Student ID]

## 4. Creative

**Prompt:**
> Write a warm, empathetic email from a student to a professor, asking for an extension on an assignment due to illness, using a genuine and heartfelt tone. Feel free to add a metaphor about how illness slowed down your progress. Keep it in the range of 120 words.

**Response:**

Subject: Extension Request - [Your Name] - [Course Name] - [Assignment Name]

Dear Professor [Professor's Last Name],

I am writing to you with a heavy heart to request a short extension for the [Assignment Name] assignment, currently due on [Original Due Date]. Unfortunately, I've been quite unwell with [briefly mention illness, e.g., a persistent flu] for the past few days.

This illness has felt like trying to navigate a dense fog, making it incredibly difficult to focus and make the progress I'd planned. I'm slowly recovering, but I'm concerned about submitting work that doesn't reflect my best effort.

Would it be possible to have an extension until [Proposed New Due Date]? I'm committed to completing this assignment to the best of my ability and appreciate your understanding.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Sincerely,

[Your Name]
[Your Student ID]
